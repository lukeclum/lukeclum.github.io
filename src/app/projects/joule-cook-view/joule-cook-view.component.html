Joule Sous Vide

Overview

In the fall of 2016 we (ChefSteps) released Joule, a connected sous vide device, to the world. But a year and a half later, the core cooking UX needed some substantial improvements.

As the first fully connected sous vide device, Joule is controlled by a companion app that allows users to set it to a specific temperature, monitor their cook, and access a host of comprehensive cooking guides. However, as the sole mechanism for controlling Joule, the app needed to provide a more stable and consistent cooking experience regardless of occasional connection issues, error cases, and multi-device scenarios.

Identifying the problems

In late 2017, I was asked to lead the redesign efforts for the Joule app. To better understand the current state of things, I began by running a qualitative study that included over 15 contextual interviews from both power users and new Joule users. Next, I did an in-depth analysis of all of our reviews to date to help quantify the main pain points in the app.

It quickly became clear to us that users were struggling with parts of the basic cooking process. There were problems with missed notifications, a lack of some core cooking information, confusion with adding food, and some general ambiguity about the sous vide process as a whole.

Moving forward, we decided that we needed to take a fresh look at the core cooking experience in the app, with an emphasis on a few goals:

  • Users should easily be able to identify the current cooking state with ease.
  • Users should feel confident about when they can add and remove their food.
  • Users should have more transparency into errors and recovery.
  • Users should be able to quickly and efficiently start a cook with limited additional interactions.
  • Users should have access to UX patterns that can grow with future products and features.

With these insights, we built a few interactive prototypes and completed a couple rounds of usability testing with both current and perspective Joule users. After a bit more design iteration we landed on a new cooking experience.

A more comprehensive cooking dashboard

Simple, Glanceable Status

To help cut through the chaos of the kitchen, we designed a simple, glanceable view that would quickly communicate the status of the cook and showcase only the most relevant information.

Detailed Cooking Stats

To help with missed notifications and meal planning we included a comprehensive timeline of cooking events.

Autostart Timers

In order to reduce unnecessary touch points, we decided to encourage users to drop their food early and let Joule automatically start their timer once their water was heated. This addressed one of our most common user complaints.

Holding Times

One of the major benefits of sous vide is the fact that you don’t have to remove your food as soon as it’s complete. Instead, it’s flexible enough to be ready when you are. To make this value more clear, we released a new feature that let users know how long they could leave their food before the results would start to change.

More Intuitive Errors

To make errors more clear and recoveries more obvious, we released a simplified pattern that helped users troubleshoot their problem and easily recover when possible.

Simple and Familiar Recipes

We simplified the recipe layout to follow more common usability patterns and be much more scannable.
